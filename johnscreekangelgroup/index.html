03
The Group

Learn to invest in startups from someone who has done it

The Johns Creek Angel Group is forming in Johns Creek, Georgia — a private circle of accomplished individuals who learn how early-stage technology investing works, then invest in it together.

It begins with education and grows into action — members who choose to can invest alongside the group, but the decision is always their own.

Led by Tuff Yen, a career venture capitalist, the group studies how professional investors source deals, conduct due diligence, structure investments, and build diversified portfolios — and puts that knowledge to work. The first gathering is a free educational seminar, with more to follow.

Direct Access

Learn from two decades of hands-on venture capital — not from a textbook.

Peer Network

Connect with accomplished professionals and entrepreneurs in the Johns Creek community.

Deal Flow Exposure

See the same kinds of opportunities institutional investors evaluate — with guidance on how to assess them.

Structured Education

A curriculum built on the real-world frameworks professional investors rely on.

What You'll Learn

The knowledge that separates successful investors from the rest

Early-stage technology investing has produced some of the greatest wealth-creation events in modern history — yet most people never learn how it actually works.

01

Deal Sourcing & Screening

Learn how professional investors find high-quality startup opportunities and quickly identify which ones are worth pursuing — and which to pass on.

02

Due Diligence

Understand how to evaluate a founding team, assess market potential, analyze financials, and investigate risks before writing a check.

03

Portfolio Strategy

Discover why diversification is the key to angel investing success and how to build a portfolio that maximizes your chances of outsized returns.

04

Wealth Creation

See how the math works — why venture capital investing, done right, should be part of every qualified investor's portfolio for long-term wealth building.

The Power Law

A few investments return the entire fund

In venture, most startups return little — and a rare few return everything. That is why diversification is the heart of portfolio strategy: enough quality shots to catch the outlier.

05
Your Host

Led by a career venture capitalist

Tuff Yen
Founder & CEO, Seraph Group
View LinkedIn Profile

Tuff Yen is a career venture capitalist and the founder of Seraph Group, an investment firm that brings together successful individuals to create wealth through ownership of private enterprises.

Mr. Yen began his career as a bioengineer at Genentech and Amgen, two of the world's leading biotechnology companies. After earning his MBA from the Yale School of Management, he entered venture capital at Chemical Venture Partners — the private equity arm of Chemical Bank (now JPMorgan) — before joining Hambrecht & Quist's affiliated fund in San Francisco.

In 2004, Mr. Yen founded Seraph Group and invented the Structured Angel Fund™ (SAF™), a proprietary venture capital fund structure designed to give angel investors and family offices the access, discipline, and diversification of an institutional venture capital firm. He has since led investments across aerospace, cybersecurity, medical devices, enterprise software, fintech, and more.

His investor network spans individuals across cities in North America, Europe, and Asia — including Fortune 500 C-level executives, entrepreneurs, technologists, and investment professionals.
